Design Principles

Rule of Thirds

NIKE

Nike.com

Divide the image into thirds, both horizontally and vertically, so you have 9 equal parts.
The important elements of your image should be placed along these lines or at the points where they intersect.

As shown in the image, the important things on the website follow the lines created by the rule of thirds.

Visual Hierarchy

Robert and Alan

Robertandalan.com

Visual hierarchy is the arrangement or presentation of elements in a way that implies importance.
It influences the order in which the human eye perceives what it sees.

This is a great example of visual hierarchy. The most important pieces of information are shown more prominently.

Contrast

Mad Men

MadMen.com

Contrast is the arrangement of opposite elements (light vs. dark colors, rough vs. smooth textures, large vs. small shapes, etc.)
in a piece so as to create visual interest, excitement, and drama.

This website is a great example of contrast. The red and white contrast perfectly and draw great attention to the page.
